# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We — as contributors, volunteers, and staff of Operation Stand Down Rhode Island — are committed to making participation in our engineering work a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, sexual identity, or veteran status.

## Our Standards

Positive behavior includes:
- Using welcoming and inclusive language
- Being respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences
- Gracefully accepting constructive criticism
- Focusing on what is best for the veteran community we serve
- Showing empathy toward other community members

Unacceptable behavior includes:
- The use of sexualized language or imagery
- Trolling, insulting/derogatory comments, personal or political attacks
- Public or private harassment
- Publishing others' private information without explicit permission
- Any conduct that would be inappropriate in a professional setting
- **Sharing veteran data inappropriately** — OSDRI handles sensitive information; treat it with the respect those who served deserve

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported to sschultz@osdri.org. All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly.

Project maintainers who do not follow or enforce the Code of Conduct in good faith may face temporary or permanent repercussions as determined by OSDRI leadership.
